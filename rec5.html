Bread Stuffed Turkey Recipe

Entertaining during the holidays can be a crazy, chaotic experience ... especially on Thanksgiving. Before you go into full-on panic mode, know we have your back (and that you got this!). With some super-simple recipes and a little planning, it's easier-than-you-think to pull off a delicious Thanksgiving meal, complete with a gorgeous bird, delicious sides, and, of course, dessert. Let's start with traditional turkey stuffing, shall we? Made with just a handful of ingredients

TOTAL TIME:
Prep: 30 min Bake: 3-3/4 hours

Ingredients


  • 6 bacon strips, diced
  • 2 cups chopped celery
  • 1 cup sliced green onions
  • 6 cups cubed corn bread
  • 6 cups cubed white bread
  • 1 can (15-1/4 ounces) whole kernel corn, undrained
  • 1-1/4 cups chicken broth
  • 3/4 cup egg substitute
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 2 teaspoons rubbed sage
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 turkey (10 to 12 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Directions
  1. In a large skillet, cook bacon over medium heat until crisp. Remove bacon to paper towels to drain. In the drippings, saute celery and onions until tender.
  2. Transfer to a large bowl. Stir in the corn bread, bread, corn, broth, egg substitute, butter, seasonings and bacon. Just before baking, loosely stuff turkey. Skewer openings; tie drumsticks together.
  3. Place on a rack in a roasting pan. Brush with oil. Bake at 325° for 3-3/4 to 4 hours or until a meat thermometer reads 180° for turkey and 165° for stuffing, basting occasionally with pan drippings. (Cover loosely with foil if turkey browns too quickly.) Yield: 8-10 servings (10 cups stuffing).
